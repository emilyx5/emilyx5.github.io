About Me

Who am I?

My name is Emily. I'm a 3rd year Engineering Physics student studying at the University of British Columbia. Throughout my degree, I continue to learn about the mechanical, software and electrical sides of engineering and the fundamental physics that govern our world. The applications of this knowledge are endless, and I enjoy the projects that come with school, work, and at home (check out the main page to see!). While exploring the many facets of engineering, I'm also interested in learning about the creative design process that comes with tackling problems — growing up with many artistic hobbies but a desire to pursue STEM, finding the intersections between a creative and technical process is what I strive for.

More about me :)

I've got quite a few hobbies that fill my free time, to varying degrees (whichever impulse strikes first...) The main hobbies are drawing, writing, lifting weights and playing the piano (I also played the flute in highschool, but unfortunately, the rental time was up and my interest went along with it...) Some of my art can be found on the main page, near the bottom; I enjoy both traditional and digital mediums, though charcoal has been a favourite as of late.
